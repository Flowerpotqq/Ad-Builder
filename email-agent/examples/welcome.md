# Welcome Email Examples

## Example 1: Wix-style Minimal Welcome

### Input Variables
```yaml
name: Sam
business_type: creator business
offer: free starter site setup
product: SiteCraft
cta_link: https://example.com/start
email_type: welcome
campaign_goal: first project launch
```

### Output Email

#### Subject
`Welcome to SiteCraft, Sam`

#### Preview Text
`You are one step away from publishing your first site.`

#### Email Body (Structured)
- Preheader: `Your workspace is ready.`
- Hero Headline: `It is your time to create`
- Hero Supporting Copy: `Build and publish your web presence with guided blocks and ready templates.`
- Primary CTA: `Start Now` -> `https://example.com/start`
- Value Block:
  - `Pick a template`
  - `Customize your brand`
  - `Publish in minutes`
- Reassurance Block: `Need help? Setup guides and support are included.`
- Closing CTA Line: `Start your first build now.`
- Footer Note: `You are receiving this because you created a SiteCraft account.`

### Why It Works
- Mirrors minimal welcome pattern with strong hero and one CTA.
- Keeps cognitive load low for first-session momentum.
- Uses progress framing to encourage immediate action.

## Example 2: SaaS Activation Welcome

### Input Variables
```yaml
name: Taylor
business_type: local business
offer: onboarding checklist
product: NAP Campaign Manager
cta_link: https://example.com/checklist
email_type: welcome
campaign_goal: activate account in first 24 hours
```

### Output Email

#### Subject
`Welcome in. Your first campaign starts here`

#### Preview Text
`Use this 3-step checklist to launch today.`

#### Email Body (Structured)
- Preheader: `Complete these 3 steps to go live.`
- Hero Headline: `You are all set to launch`
- Hero Supporting Copy: `Follow a short setup path to capture leads and send your first campaign.`
- Primary CTA: `Open My Checklist` -> `https://example.com/checklist`
- Steps Block:
  1. `Add your sender domain`
  2. `Import your lead list`
  3. `Schedule your first campaign`
- Reassurance Block: `Most teams complete setup in under 20 minutes.`
- Closing CTA Line: `Start with step 1 now.`
- Footer Note: `You signed up for NAP Campaign Manager.`

### Why It Works
- Clear first win and clear path reduce drop-off.
- Numeric steps improve perceived simplicity.
- CTA points to an activation asset, not generic homepage.

## Example 4: Product-led Welcome with Guided Tour

### Input Variables
```yaml
name: Devon
business_type: ecommerce operator
offer: guided product tour
product: FlowCart
cta_link: https://example.com/tour
email_type: welcome
campaign_goal: feature discovery
```

### Output Email

#### Subject
`Devon, your FlowCart workspace is live`

#### Preview Text
`Take a 2-minute guided tour to unlock your first automation.`

#### Email Body (Structured)
- Preheader: `Your dashboard is ready to configure.`
- Hero Headline: `Start with a quick tour`
- Hero Supporting Copy: `Learn where to launch your first flow and how to measure results.`
- Primary CTA: `Start Guided Tour` -> `https://example.com/tour`
- Feature Highlights:
  - `Abandoned cart flow templates`
  - `Revenue attribution dashboard`
  - `Segment-based targeting`
- Reassurance Block: `Tour takes about 2 minutes and can be paused anytime.`
- Closing CTA Line: `Take the tour and launch your first flow today.`
- Footer Note: `You created a FlowCart account.`

### Why It Works
- Emphasizes fast setup and low effort.
- Aligns CTA to first product behavior that predicts retention.
- Reduces anxiety with clear time estimate.
