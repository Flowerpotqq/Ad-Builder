# Components Library

Reusable blocks for campaign emails.
Each component includes usage, copy pattern, and formatting constraints.

## 1) Hero Section

When to use:
- Always. It anchors intent within first screen.

Copy pattern:
- Eyebrow (optional): category cue
- Headline: primary promise
- Supporting line: one sentence explanation
- CTA: one action

Example copy:
- Eyebrow: `Welcome to NAP Solutions`
- Headline: `Never miss another lead`
- Supporting: `Automate replies and bookings while you focus on service delivery.`
- CTA: `Book A Free Demo`

Formatting rules:
- Headline <= 9 words preferred
- Support copy <= 22 words
- Do not stack more than one CTA in hero

## 2) Feature Grid (NAP-style)

When to use:
- Productized service bundles
- Multi-feature launches
- Capability education

Copy pattern per card:
- Category label
- Feature title
- Outcome sentence

Example card:
- Label: `VOICE AI`
- Title: `AI Receptionist`
- Body: `Answers every call, qualifies leads, and books appointments automatically.`

Formatting rules:
- 2 or 3 columns desktop equivalent; stack on mobile
- 3 to 6 cards ideal
- Keep card body <= 18 words

## 3) Primary CTA Button

When to use:
- Once per email as main conversion action.

Example copy:
- `Start Free Trial`
- `Book My Demo`
- `Activate 20% Savings`

Formatting rules:
- Verb-led label
- Avoid generic words: `Click Here`
- Place near value proof and again near end only if email is long

## 4) Social Proof Block

When to use:
- Mid or lower funnel audiences needing confidence.

Copy pattern:
- Proof headline
- Quant metric or testimonial
- Source line

Example copy:
- Headline: `Trusted by 400+ local service teams`
- Proof: `Teams using automated follow-up recovered 31% more missed inquiries in 60 days.`
- Source: `Based on internal customer outcomes, Q1 2026 cohort.`

Formatting rules:
- One strong datapoint beats many weak claims
- If no reliable metric exists, use brief testimonial quote

## 5) Offer Details Strip

When to use:
- Promotional and re-engagement offers.

Example copy:
- `Offer: 20% off annual plan`
- `Window: Ends Friday at 11:59 PM ET`
- `Eligibility: New upgrades only`

Formatting rules:
- Make terms explicit
- Keep text compact and unambiguous

## 6) Steps Block (Onboarding)

When to use:
- Welcome and activation campaigns.

Example copy:
1. Connect your inbox
2. Add your service hours
3. Enable booking rules

Formatting rules:
- 2 to 4 steps
- Start with verbs
- Keep each step under one line if possible

## 7) FAQ Micro-block

When to use:
- Any email where objections block action.

Example copy:
- `Need help setting up?`
- `Our team can configure your first workflow in one call.`

Formatting rules:
- Max 2 mini FAQs per email
- Keep responses under 20 words

## 8) Footer

When to use:
- Always.

Must include:
- Company identity
- Reason recipient got the email
- Unsubscribe or preference link
- Contact or support path

Formatting rules:
- Keep neutral tone
- Do not hide compliance links
