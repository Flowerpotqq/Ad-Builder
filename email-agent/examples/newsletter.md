# Newsletter Email Examples

## Example 1: NAP Solutions-style SaaS Feature Email

### Input Variables
```yaml
name: Alex
business_type: local service company
offer: new automation suite release
product: NAP Solutions Platform
cta_link: https://example.com/demo
email_type: newsletter
campaign_goal: feature education + demo bookings
```

### Output Email

#### Subject
`New in NAP: 7 AI services, one workflow`

#### Preview Text
`See how AI Receptionist, Outbound, and Email Automation now work together.`

#### Email Body (Structured)
- Preheader: `A practical update for teams that want faster lead response.`
- Hero Headline: `Never miss a lead again`
- Hero Supporting Copy: `Our new service stack connects calls, follow-up, website chat, and campaign automation in one flow.`
- Primary CTA: `Book A Free Demo` -> `https://example.com/demo`
- Feature Grid:
  - `VOICE AI | AI Receptionist | Answers calls 24/7 and books appointments.`
  - `VOICE AI | Outbound Agent | Follows up and re-engages unanswered leads.`
  - `DIGITAL PRESENCE | Web Development | Conversion-focused pages for local services.`
  - `WEBSITE AI | AI Chat Widget | Qualifies visitors and routes high-intent leads.`
  - `E-COMMERCE | FBA Automation | Automates inventory and order workflows.`
  - `MARKETING | Email Automation | Sends nurture, onboarding, and recall campaigns.`
- Proof Block: `Teams combining inbound and follow-up automation see fewer missed opportunities.`
- Reassurance Block: `Setup included. Cancel anytime.`
- Closing CTA Line: `See which workflow fits your business in a short demo.`
- Footer Note: `You receive this monthly product update as a NAP subscriber.`

### Why It Works
- Uses card-based modular structure for multi-feature clarity.
- Keeps headline outcome-driven and broad.
- Balances many features with one central CTA.

## Example 2: Educational Newsletter

### Input Variables
```yaml
name: Morgan
business_type: agency
offer: weekly optimization insights
product: CampaignOps Weekly
cta_link: https://example.com/read
email_type: newsletter
campaign_goal: content engagement
```

### Output Email

#### Subject
`3 campaign wins you can apply this week`

#### Preview Text
`Short plays for better reply rates and cleaner pipeline handoffs.`

#### Email Body (Structured)
- Preheader: `This week: subject tests, follow-up timing, and CTA clarity.`
- Hero Headline: `Your weekly campaign playbook`
- Hero Supporting Copy: `Three practical updates to improve performance without adding new tools.`
- Primary CTA: `Read This Week's Plays` -> `https://example.com/read`
- Content Modules:
  - `Subject test: benefit-led lines outperformed generic urgency in this segment.`
  - `Timing: follow-up at 18-24 hours improved response rates for warm leads.`
  - `CTA copy: action-specific labels drove more qualified clicks.`
- Reassurance Block: `Each issue is written for implementation in under 15 minutes.`
- Closing CTA Line: `Open the full breakdown.`
- Footer Note: `You subscribed to CampaignOps Weekly updates.`

### Why It Works
- Delivers immediate utility, improving long-term engagement.
- Modular structure supports fast scanning.
- Single featured CTA avoids click dilution.

## Example 4: Product + Content Hybrid Newsletter

### Input Variables
```yaml
name: Jamie
business_type: ecommerce
offer: new dashboard widgets + tutorial
product: InsightCart
cta_link: https://example.com/update
email_type: newsletter
campaign_goal: product adoption
```

### Output Email

#### Subject
`New in InsightCart: faster campaign diagnostics`

#### Preview Text
`Use the new widgets to spot drop-offs and fix flows faster.`

#### Email Body (Structured)
- Preheader: `Dashboard update plus a 5-minute walkthrough.`
- Hero Headline: `Find and fix flow bottlenecks faster`
- Hero Supporting Copy: `New widgets reveal where conversion drops so your team can optimize in fewer steps.`
- Primary CTA: `See The Update` -> `https://example.com/update`
- Update Modules:
  - `Widget 1: Segment-level conversion drop-off`
  - `Widget 2: Time-to-follow-up trend`
  - `Widget 3: Revenue impact by automation`
- Reassurance Block: `Tutorial includes setup guide and best-practice defaults.`
- Closing CTA Line: `Open the update and run your first diagnostic.`
- Footer Note: `You receive product updates from InsightCart.`

### Why It Works
- Links product changes to user outcomes, not feature jargon.
- Drives adoption with educational context.
- Maintains one primary click path.
