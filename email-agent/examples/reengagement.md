# Re-engagement Email Examples

## Example 1: Soft Return Nudge

### Input Variables
```yaml
name: Avery
business_type: SaaS
offer: return to unfinished setup
product: LaunchPilot
cta_link: https://example.com/resume
email_type: reengagement
campaign_goal: reactivation
```

### Output Email

#### Subject
`Avery, want to pick up where you left off?`

#### Preview Text
`Your setup is still saved and takes about 3 minutes to finish.`

#### Email Body (Structured)
- Preheader: `No restart needed. Your progress is saved.`
- Hero Headline: `Your workspace is waiting`
- Hero Supporting Copy: `You are close to launch. Finish setup and start running campaigns this week.`
- Primary CTA: `Resume Setup` -> `https://example.com/resume`
- Return Value Block:
  - `Saved drafts are still available`
  - `New templates added since your last visit`
  - `Guided checklist for quick completion`
- Reassurance Block: `Need help? We can walk you through setup in one call.`
- Closing CTA Line: `Return and go live in minutes.`
- Footer Note: `You are receiving this because your account has been inactive.`

### Why It Works
- Acknowledges inactivity without blame.
- Uses saved-progress framing to reduce restart anxiety.
- Offers a simple, low-friction reentry.

## Example 2: Feature Update Re-engagement

### Input Variables
```yaml
name: Lee
business_type: local business
offer: new missed-call recovery feature
product: NAP Inbox
cta_link: https://example.com/return
email_type: reengagement
campaign_goal: return logins
```

### Output Email

#### Subject
`Since your last login: new missed-call recovery`

#### Preview Text
`Capture more leads automatically with the new callback flow.`

#### Email Body (Structured)
- Preheader: `We shipped updates designed for faster follow-up.`
- Hero Headline: `A better way to recover missed calls`
- Hero Supporting Copy: `New callback automation routes missed inquiries to instant follow-up sequences.`
- Primary CTA: `See What Is New` -> `https://example.com/return`
- New Value Block:
  - `Auto callback prompts`
  - `Lead priority scoring`
  - `One-click booking links`
- Reassurance Block: `Your existing settings remain unchanged.`
- Closing CTA Line: `Log in and test the new flow today.`
- Footer Note: `You are receiving product updates as an account holder.`

### Why It Works
- Gives a concrete reason to come back (new value).
- Ties update to user pain point.
- Maintains low-pressure tone while driving action.

## Example 3: Incentive-based Win-back

### Input Variables
```yaml
name: Quinn
business_type: ecommerce
offer: 14-day premium reactivation pass
product: CartLift
cta_link: https://example.com/reactivate
email_type: reengagement
campaign_goal: trial reactivation
```

### Output Email

#### Subject
`Come back with a 14-day premium pass`

#### Preview Text
`Reactivate now and test advanced flows at no extra cost.`

#### Email Body (Structured)
- Preheader: `Premium features unlocked for 14 days after reactivation.`
- Hero Headline: `Pick up momentum with premium access`
- Hero Supporting Copy: `Restart your account and use premium automation to recover more carts this month.`
- Primary CTA: `Reactivate My Account` -> `https://example.com/reactivate`
- Offer Details:
  - `Pass Length: 14 days`
  - `Includes: advanced segmentation + AI timing`
  - `Expires: April 20, 2026`
- Reassurance Block: `No long-term commitment required to test.`
- Closing CTA Line: `Reactivate now while your pass is available.`
- Footer Note: `You are receiving this as a previous CartLift trial user.`

### Why It Works
- Combines urgency with low-risk return.
- Makes incentive clear and bounded.
- Keeps CTA tightly aligned to offer.

## Example 4: Preference-based Final Nudge

### Input Variables
```yaml
name: Blair
business_type: SaaS
offer: final check-in with frequency options
product: MetricLane
cta_link: https://example.com/preferences
email_type: reengagement
campaign_goal: engagement or clean list hygiene
```

### Output Email

#### Subject
`Should we keep sending MetricLane updates?`

#### Preview Text
`Choose weekly, monthly, or pause with one click.`

#### Email Body (Structured)
- Preheader: `We want your inbox preferences to match your needs.`
- Hero Headline: `Set your update preferences`
- Hero Supporting Copy: `If timing has been off, choose a lighter cadence instead of unsubscribing entirely.`
- Primary CTA: `Update My Preferences` -> `https://example.com/preferences`
- Options Block:
  - `Weekly product updates`
  - `Monthly summary only`
  - `Pause for 60 days`
- Reassurance Block: `You stay in control of what you receive.`
- Closing CTA Line: `Set your preference in under a minute.`
- Footer Note: `You can unsubscribe anytime.`

### Why It Works
- Protects list quality while reducing hard churn.
- Gives recipient control, improving trust.
- Uses gentle tone suitable for late-stage re-engagement.
