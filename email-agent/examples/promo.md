# Promotional Email Examples

## Example 1: DocuSign-style Direct Offer (20% off)

### Input Variables
```yaml
name: Jordan
business_type: B2B software
offer: 20% off annual Business plan
product: SignFlow Pro
cta_link: https://example.com/upgrade
email_type: promotional
campaign_goal: annual upgrades
```

### Output Email

#### Subject
`Lock in 20% savings on SignFlow Pro`

#### Preview Text
`Upgrade to annual by Friday and save instantly.`

#### Email Body (Structured)
- Preheader: `Your annual discount expires Friday at 11:59 PM ET.`
- Hero Headline: `Upgrade now and save 20%`
- Hero Supporting Copy: `Get full e-sign workflows, team controls, and priority support at a lower annual rate.`
- Primary CTA: `Activate 20% Savings` -> `https://example.com/upgrade`
- Offer Details Block:
  - `Offer: 20% off annual Business plan`
  - `Deadline: Friday 11:59 PM ET`
  - `Eligibility: Current monthly subscribers`
- Reassurance Block: `No data migration needed. Your settings stay exactly as they are.`
- Closing CTA Line: `Switch once, save all year.`
- Footer Note: `You received this because you manage a SignFlow Pro subscription.`

### Why It Works
- Strong first-screen clarity: offer + deadline + action.
- Uses loss aversion without exaggerated fear language.
- Terms are explicit, reducing post-click confusion.

## Example 2: Limited Seat Promo for Demo Pipeline

### Input Variables
```yaml
name: Priya
business_type: local services
offer: free setup for first 25 teams
product: NAP Solutions AI Receptionist
cta_link: https://example.com/demo
email_type: promotional
campaign_goal: demo bookings
```

### Output Email

#### Subject
`25 teams get free setup this week`

#### Preview Text
`If missed calls are costing bookings, this is your fastest fix.`

#### Email Body (Structured)
- Preheader: `Free setup slots close when all 25 are claimed.`
- Hero Headline: `Turn missed calls into booked jobs`
- Hero Supporting Copy: `AI Receptionist answers instantly, qualifies leads, and books appointments while your team is busy.`
- Primary CTA: `Book My Free Setup Demo` -> `https://example.com/demo`
- Feature Snapshot:
  - `24/7 lead capture`
  - `Smart qualification`
  - `Calendar booking sync`
- Proof Block: `Service teams using this flow reported fewer dropped inquiries in the first month.`
- Reassurance Block: `Setup included. Cancel anytime.`
- Closing CTA Line: `Claim your slot before this week closes.`
- Footer Note: `You are receiving this because you requested lead automation updates.`

### Why It Works
- Outcome-first headline tied directly to business pain.
- Scarcity mechanism is concrete (25 slots) and believable.
- CTA is specific and low-friction.

## Example 3: Dollar-value Offer Variant

### Input Variables
```yaml
name: Alexis
business_type: ecommerce
offer: $300 onboarding credit
product: CartLift Automation
cta_link: https://example.com/start
email_type: promotional
campaign_goal: trial to paid conversion
```

### Output Email

#### Subject
`Get a $300 onboarding credit this month`

#### Preview Text
`Launch your recovery flows with implementation help included.`

#### Email Body (Structured)
- Preheader: `Credit applies to first month setup services.`
- Hero Headline: `$300 credit for faster launch`
- Hero Supporting Copy: `Move from trial to live recovery flows with hands-on onboarding support.`
- Primary CTA: `Claim My Onboarding Credit` -> `https://example.com/start`
- Offer Details:
  - `Applies to: new annual plans`
  - `Window: through April 30`
  - `Includes: flow setup and first optimization review`
- Reassurance Block: `No hidden fees. Credit is applied on your first invoice.`
- Closing CTA Line: `Launch now and recover revenue sooner.`
- Footer Note: `You are receiving this as an active CartLift trial user.`

### Why It Works
- Dollar framing can feel more concrete than percent framing.
- Reduces activation friction by bundling support.
- Keeps structure simple with one conversion path.

## Example 4: Seasonal Promo with Urgency

### Input Variables
```yaml
name: Marcus
business_type: agency
offer: spring campaign bundle expires in 72 hours
product: PipelinePilot
cta_link: https://example.com/bundle
email_type: promotional
campaign_goal: package sales
```

### Output Email

#### Subject
`72 hours left: Spring growth bundle`

#### Preview Text
`Get campaign automation + reporting in one package before it closes.`

#### Email Body (Structured)
- Preheader: `Final 72-hour window for Spring bundle pricing.`
- Hero Headline: `Your spring campaign stack, bundled`
- Hero Supporting Copy: `Launch outbound, follow-up, and reporting from one dashboard with one discounted plan.`
- Primary CTA: `Activate Spring Bundle` -> `https://example.com/bundle`
- Included Modules:
  - `Outbound sequence builder`
  - `Lead response automation`
  - `Weekly performance snapshots`
- Proof Block: `Agencies use bundled workflows to reduce handoff delays and speed campaign launches.`
- Reassurance Block: `Implementation template included on day one.`
- Closing CTA Line: `Offer closes in 72 hours.`
- Footer Note: `You opted in for product and pricing updates.`

### Why It Works
- Deadline is prominent but not aggressive.
- Package clarity reduces decision fatigue.
- CTA language matches buyer intent (activate bundle).
