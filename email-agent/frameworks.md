# Frameworks: Campaign Email Types

Source baseline: Twilio industry guidance on email types and use-cases (article title: "21 Types of Emails Explained (With Tips & Examples)", published August 2, 2025).
Applied here only for the 5 campaign classes used by this system.

---

## 1) Promotional Email Framework

Purpose:
- Drive immediate action on an offer, deal, event, or limited opportunity.

When to use:
- Time-bound discounts
- New customer incentives
- Seasonal pushes
- Sales activation windows

Step-by-step structure:
1. Subject: benefit-led curiosity or direct value
2. Preview: clarify offer condition quickly
3. Hero: offer headline and short proof statement
4. Offer details: what, for whom, by when
5. Friction reducer: setup included, cancel anytime, no code needed
6. CTA: one high-clarity action
7. Footer: trust and compliance

Psychological triggers:
- Scarcity (time-limited window)
- Loss aversion (missed outcome)
- Value framing (effort vs reward)

CTA strategy:
- Primary CTA must match offer verb exactly
- Avoid vague labels like "Learn More" for hard offers
- Add one urgency support line near CTA

## 2) Welcome Email Framework

Purpose:
- Turn sign-up intent into first meaningful action.

When to use:
- New account creation
- Trial starts
- Newsletter subscription
- New lead magnet subscriber

Step-by-step structure:
1. Subject: warm confirmation + expected value
2. Preview: immediate next step
3. Hero: identity confirmation ("you are in")
4. Value promise: 2-3 outcomes they can achieve
5. Getting-started steps: 2-4 short steps
6. CTA: start first action now
7. Footer: support link and expectation setting

Psychological triggers:
- Commitment consistency (continue what they started)
- Progress effect (small first win)
- Belonging (you joined a community/platform)

CTA strategy:
- Action should complete first success milestone
- Example: "Build Your First Campaign" not "Explore"

## 3) Newsletter Email Framework

Purpose:
- Build relationship and authority while driving repeat traffic or product engagement.

When to use:
- Weekly or monthly updates
- Product tips and educational content
- Customer stories and highlights

Step-by-step structure:
1. Subject: topic value and relevance
2. Preview: strongest insight or featured item
3. Intro: concise editorial context
4. Content modules: 3-5 blocks max
5. Featured CTA: one primary click destination
6. Secondary links: optional, clearly grouped
7. Footer: preference management and social links

Psychological triggers:
- Utility (practical value)
- Curiosity (what changed, what is next)
- Social proof (customer examples)

CTA strategy:
- One featured CTA; additional links should not compete visually
- Use language tied to content outcome

---

## 4) Transactional Email Framework

Purpose:
- Confirm critical account or purchase events while preserving trust.

When to use:
- Order confirmation
- Receipt and invoice
- Password reset
- Appointment reminder
- Delivery or status updates

Step-by-step structure:
1. Subject: explicit event confirmation
2. Preview: key status at a glance
3. Header: event summary and timestamp
4. Core details: itemized and clear
5. Next step CTA: track, manage, or review
6. Help path: support contact and FAQ
7. Footer: legal and account preferences

Psychological triggers:
- Certainty (clear status)
- Control (what to do next)
- Trust reinforcement (accurate details)

CTA strategy:
- Functional and direct: "Track Package", "Reset Password"
- No promotional clutter in critical system notifications

---

## 5) Re-engagement Email Framework

Purpose:
- Reactivate inactive users with a meaningful reason to return.

When to use:
- No login or no purchase windows
- Dormant trial users
- Cold subscribers

Step-by-step structure:
1. Subject: personalized return reason
2. Preview: what changed since last engagement
3. Hero: acknowledge inactivity without guilt tone
4. New value section: feature, outcome, or offer
5. Incentive (optional): limited-time win-back offer
6. CTA: resume one simple action
7. Preference split: pause, reduce frequency, or unsubscribe

Psychological triggers:
- Fresh start framing
- FOMO on missed improvements
- Ease of return (low effort)

CTA strategy:
- Use "Resume" and "Pick up where you left off" style language
- Add a low-commitment fallback link

---

## Cross-framework Rules
- Start every email by answering:
  - Why this message now?
  - Why this recipient?
  - What single action should happen next?
- Keep one conversion priority per email.
- If multiple actions are needed, sequence across multiple emails, not one overcrowded email.
